Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi was a prominent Indian leader who played a key role in India's struggle for independence from British rule.

He was known for his principles of non-violence and civil disobedience, which he called Satyagraha.

Some key facts about Mahatma Gandhi:

  • Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India.
  • Assassinated on January 30, 1948, in New Delhi, India.
  • Advocated for the rights of the poor and oppressed.
  • Played a significant role in negotiations for India's independence in 1947.
